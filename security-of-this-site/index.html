Authenticity

Git commits belonging to me (as of April 2021) are signed via my Split GPG setup with a dedicated subkey that was generated by my Master Keyring. Said subkey has its own VM (with no networking whatsoever). Also, said Git commits are pushed via my Split SSH setup. My Master Keyring (OpenPGP (rsa4096)) and private key database (KeePass 2 (stores my ssh-ed25519 secret key)) both securely reside in my vault.

Whilst an issued TLS certificate associated with its respective website (e.g. HTTPS) is usually enough for authenticity (e.g. preventing MITM attacks), it does not mitigate comprehensive attack vectors such as mistakenly issued certificates and CA breaching (server-side), and certificate store poisoning (client-side).

Still, most browsers don't have pinned certificates, nor do many servers support key pinning. The modern web is built upon trusted roots; whilst it does have its downsides, it does however prevent sysadmins from any potential misconfiguration of their key pinning.

Due to the aformentioned, I implore you to verify this website (contents of /) and any of my other wares using the information provided.

Audit

If you're viewing the HTML5 version of my site, you can audit it via the Lighthouse tool that is bundled in Chromium-based browsers (I recommend ungoogled-chromium). Lighthouse supports testing with device emulation, i.e. to execute mobile mode on a desktop machine or vice versa.

Use of Incognito mode or even better; a DisposableVM (if on Qubes OS) or Microsoft Defender Application Guard (if on Windows 10 (Hyper-V is a prerequisite)) — is at your own discretion.
